1B · The Value System

01There is an answer to every situation.
"In any situation you can find a good answer."

Instilled watching senior bankers on a Bombay treasury floor. He will never publish a pure complaint post without a path through it.

02Admiration and imitation are not the same thing.
"I admired it, but I could never embrace... I should know what I should impress and what I should not."

Governs negotiations, feedback, any conversation not going his way. His posts will not wrap up cleanly. The truth is messier and he respects that.

04Institutional requirement supersedes individual aspiration — and that is not a bad thing.
"Institutional requirement supersedes individual aspiration is given, which over a period of time one realizes is the reality of life."

He has been on both sides of the decision. He does not hold bitterness. This makes him credible to anyone who has been passed over and needs the system explained without being told the system is wrong.

05Every professional has a core.
"One person's core was discipline... another's was conversation. There is a difference between the two."

His own core is complexity, not sales. Content must reflect specificity about professional identity — not generic 'work harder' narratives.

"I keep my bias. It is that we will all evaluate things from the perspective which we have seen in life."

He built a correction system around his own bias. He will never frame bias as a moral failing — only as a structural feature requiring a practical workaround.

Chapter 03

The Voice Bible

LinkedIn content fails most often not from bad ideas but from a voice the client cannot recognise as their own. These are structural rules derived from how he actually speaks, not aesthetic preferences.

Track B — Provisional. Derived from spoken language, not written output. Validate all rules against the first five posts before treating as fixed.

The 7 Voice Rules

01

Earn the Lesson Through the Story

The insight is never stated first. The story arrives first. The lesson is what the story forces.

Applies to all post types.

WrongGreat leaders create psychological safety. Here's how to build it on your team.
RightThere was a meeting. I was the most junior person in the room. My boss stepped out, looked back, and only asked me to come along. He didn't say a word about trust. But I understood exactly what he was telling me.
02

Name People by Their Core Quality, Not Their Title

Every person is identified by what they are, not what they do.

Applies every time a person (anonymised) is introduced.

WrongMy former boss at a leading private sector bank taught me about discipline.
RightI had a boss once — one of my closest friends today — who was discipline itself. Whatever wild example of professional discipline you can think of, he had it covered.
03

Include the Failure of Absorption

Bharat explicitly names what he could not learn from the people he most admired. This is structural, not performative.

Applies to any post involving a mentor, boss, or formative influence.

WrongHe taught me the power of discipline and I have practised it every day since.
RightI tried. I genuinely tried to build what he had. And I couldn't. My mind goes deep on one thing until it's solved, and that is not what organised discipline looks like. Eventually, I stopped fighting it.
04

Ground Every Metaphor in the Physical World

Analogies are always concrete and tactile. His best: closing a deal is like finding a water valve in a Rajasthani desert and drinking.

Applies to any post about the craft or feeling of work.

WrongClosing a deal is about aligning stakeholders toward a shared outcome.
RightPicture a desert. No tap. You're thirsty in the way that makes everything else irrelevant. You find a valve. You pull. Water comes. That's what closing a transaction feels like.
05

Offer the Counterargument to Your Own Position

He does not argue one side and leave. He complicates his own positions.

Applies to any post making a claim about professional success or leadership.

WrongDiscipline is the only thing that separates good professionals from great ones.
RightDiscipline is one kind of excellence. Perfection is another. Navigation is a third. They are not interchangeable. An institution needs all three — just not from the same person.
06

Write as if Telling One Person a Story Over Coffee

Intimate conversation, not public address.

Applies to all posts. Any post that sounds like a speech has broken this rule.

WrongLeaders who master the art of feedback create high-performance cultures.
RightHe forwarded me something. Something the bank had done well. My first response — and I am a little embarrassed by this — was to say, I did that four months ago. He let that sit. Then he said: it's not about the trumpet.
07

Close on Truth, Not Motivation

The close is quiet and specific. It lands with weight, not volume.

Applies to every post without exception.

WrongBecause at the end of the day, the best investment you can make is in yourself.
RightI still have that feedback. Ten years later. Not because I followed every word of it. Because it was the most honest thing anyone put on paper about who I was at that age.

Voice Don'ts

"At the end of the day..." — filler bridge "Game-changer" — marketing register "I've always believed..." — states belief before the story earns it "Here are 5 things great leaders do..." — listicle framing "The key takeaway is..." — announces the lesson early "In today's fast-paced world..." — violates every rule at once "My mentor always said..." — skips failure-of-absorption "Success is a journey..." — wisdom-poster register "Trust the process" — unqualified, no counterargument "I am passionate about..." — pitch deck language "Excited to share..." — performed, not described "We need to talk about..." — announces, doesn't arrive "The banking industry..." as a monolith — he thinks in people Clean resolution endings — not honest enough Performative humility — false modesty

Voice Dos

Open with a scene — a person, a moment, a room Use "so" as a connector from event to meaning Anonymise people with texture, not labels Include what he couldn't do, every time State the comfortable reading, then name what it misses Let physical metaphors run their full course Name institution type, never the institution End on a quiet specific moment Use second person sparingly, so it lands Return to the opening image at the close State the failed attempt before the success Complicate the lesson before closing on it Use precise numbers — "four months ago," "six people" Write about people unlike him — that's where self-knowledge lives Let a brief digression exist — it builds intimacy

Chapter 05

The 5 Post Types

Not all content does the same job. Without defined post types, the writer defaults to whatever feels natural that week — and the mix becomes accidental rather than strategic.

Type 01 · Recognition

The Person Portrait

Introduce one real person (anonymised) who changed how Bharat sees something. Core quality in physical detail, failure-of-absorption clause, what transferred and what didn't.

Frequency — 1/week months 1–3; 1/fortnight after.

"The most disciplined professional I have ever met could not explain how he did it — and what I finally stopped trying to copy."

Never combine with a list or advice post.
Type 02 · Relief

The Institutional Truth

Explain how organisations actually work — decisions, promotions, biases, structures. Situation → gap → lens → honest limitation.

Frequency — 1/fortnight.

"When I was passed over for a role I had prepared for, my first reaction was: why? Three years later, sitting on the other side of that decision, I understood."

Never combine with The Person Portrait.
Type 03 · Appetite

The Leadership Craft Post

Make the interior work of developing people visible. Specific moment → craft distinction → what the reader can carry into their next conversation.

Frequency — 1/fortnight.

"The best feedback I ever gave took thirty seconds. The worst took an hour. Here's what I eventually understood about the difference."

Never combine with regulatory or institutional commentary.
Type 04 · Clarity

The Career Lens

A specific, non-motivational way to read a career situation. Situation → what's beneath the surface → the reframe → what changes.

Frequency — 1/week early months — highest-engagement type.

"You can admire a quality without being able to absorb it. These are not the same thing."

Never combine with Person Portrait unless purely illustrating the lens.
Type 05 · Orientation

The Live Take

Connect Bharat's expertise to something happening now. Trend described (never editorialised at policy level) → connected to lived expertise → one specific position.

Frequency — Maximum 1/month, triggered by live signal, never scheduled.

"Everyone is talking about Gen Z not wanting to stay. Nobody is talking about what we are actually offering when they arrive."

Never combine with regulatory commentary, named institutions, market calls.

Chapter 06

Post Anatomy — The Master Template

Structure is not a constraint on creativity — it's what makes creativity repeatable. A writer who internalises this template can produce posts quickly without sacrificing quality.

Section 1 — The Hook

Lines 1–2 combined: ≤210 characters. Blank line after line 2 is mandatory, every time. Never in hook: "I want to share...", yes/no questions, bullet points, emoji (confirm with client), hashtags.

Hook TypeExample
Scene-drop (primary)"There was a meeting. I was the most junior person in the room."
Reversal"The best feedback I ever received wasn't from a boss who liked me."
Specific Number"Twenty-five years later, I still have the email."
Confession"For years, I confused admiring a quality with absorbing one. They are not the same thing."

Section 2 — The Body

Must be specific: core quality (never named), career stage, exact moment, what was at stake. Every body must have a moment where the outcome is not guaranteed. Max 3 lines per paragraph, blank line between every paragraph.

Section 3 — The Pivot

Bridges story to insight. Approved constructions:

  • ✓"And that's the thing I didn't understand then, but I understand now."
  • ✓"I've thought about that conversation a lot since then."
  • ✓"It took me X years to understand what actually happened there."
  • ✓"What I didn't know at the time was..."
  • ✓"The story isn't really about X. It's about Y."

Section 4 — The Framework (Optional)

Use in Institutional Truth and Career Lens posts only. Never in Person Portrait. Rarely in Leadership Craft. 3–4 bullets maximum. No two bullets start with the same word.

Section 5 — The Close

Option A — Truth Statement

One sentence naming what is true. Weight, not uplift.

"The most honest feedback I ever received wasn't the kindest. But it was the one that lasted. And there is no substitute for that."
Option B — Stakes Question

One question that opens rather than closes.

"If you're wondering why the people around you are moving faster — before you ask what they have that you don't, ask whether you actually want what they have, or what you think it represents."
Never use both close types in the same post.

Section 6 — Hashtags

3–5 maximum, end of post after a blank line. 1 broad tag, 1–2 topic-specific, 1 personal brand tag (develop after first 5 posts). No hashtags in body text.

Confirm with Bharat whether he wants hashtags at all.

Type 01

Authenticity Conflict

"This doesn't sound like me."

Return to the Voice Bible. If it follows the rules, explain which and why. If it violates one, rewrite immediately. If it follows rules but is still rejected — the Bible needs updating.

If it reflects genuine voice, accept and update the Bible. If it makes the post more generic, say so once, then comply. Do not repeat the objection.

Chapter 08

The Editing Protocol

Editing is not proofreading. Each pass has a single focus. Combining passes produces posts that pass no standard rigorously.

Pass 01

The Truth Pass

Specificity and factual grounding only.

  • ✓Every person's core quality is traceable to transcript or validated story call
  • ✓Every number is grounded in what Bharat actually said
  • ✓No extrapolation beyond the brief
  • ✓All institution references are anonymised
  • ✓No client or counterparty identifiable
  • ✓The insight actually follows from the story
Pass 02

The Voice Pass

Voice Bible compliance only.

  • ✓No phrase from Voice DON'TS appears anywhere
  • ✓Post opens with a scene, not a claim
  • ✓Failure-of-absorption clause present where relevant
  • ✓All people identified by core quality, not title
  • ✓Close avoids motivation — lands on truth or question
  • ✓At least one Sentence Signature Table construction present
Pass 03

The Rhythm Pass

Structure and pacing only.

  • ✓Read aloud — mark every hesitation, rewrite it
  • ✓Hook ≤210 characters on lines 1–2
  • ✓Blank line after line 2, and after every paragraph
  • ✓No paragraph longer than 3 lines
  • ✓Pivot uses an approved construction
  • ✓Last sentence feels complete read alone
Pass 04

The Business Pass

Strategic intent only.

  • ✓Post does what the brief said it would do
  • ✓Reaches the target persona — fear or desire felt
  • ✓Named professional objective served
  • ✓Post does not do what the brief prohibited
  • ✓No Hard Constraint violated
Section C (Business Pass) failure: post returns to writer. No client review. No exceptions.

Sequencing Rules

  • ✓Never follow a Person Portrait directly with an Institutional Truth — buffer with a Career Lens
  • ✓A Leadership Craft Post earns the right to be followed by a more assertive position
  • ✓Never stack two high-vulnerability posts — follow with Craft or Career Lens
  • ✓Never open a week with a Live Take

Topic Rotation

A topic can be revisited after 8 posts on other subjects (~3–4 weeks). Earlier, only as a sequel. Repetition says the same thing again; a sequel goes somewhere the original did not — a specific case, a counter-example, a time-elapsed update.

Chapter 10

Quality Control Gates

The Editing Protocol improves a post. This gate decides whether it's ready to leave the writer's hands — a different question entirely.

A · Voice & Identity

  • ✓Could only have been written by Bharat — swap his name for any other banker; if it still works, it fails
  • ✓No phrase from Voice DON'TS appears anywhere
  • ✓At least one Sentence Signature Table construction present
  • ✓Emotional temperature matches his transcript register
  • ✓No claim contradicts the Value System (Ch. 01B)

B · Structure & Rhythm

  • ✓Hook ≤210 characters on lines 1–2
  • ✓Blank line rule followed throughout
  • ✓Pivot present, approved construction used
  • ✓Close is Truth Statement or Stakes Question — not both, not neither
  • ✓Reads fluently aloud, no re-reading needed

C · Business Logic

  • ✓Serves a named professional objective
  • ✓Reaches the named primary persona — fear or desire felt
  • ✓Does exactly what the brief specified
  • ✓Does not do what the brief prohibited
  • ✓No Hard Constraint violated

Quality Gate Rule

Any failure in Section C = post returns to the writer immediately. No client review. No exceptions. Failures in Section A or B = targeted fix — not a full rewrite unless the failure is structural.

What this document cannot do is replace the client's review — not because it's incomplete, but because Bharat is the only person who knows when something feels wrong in a way he cannot yet articulate.
